Goodbye to Le Wagon Zurich! 🎓👋🚀

Last week, we had our last hashtag#DemoDay at Le Wagon Zurich. A big shout-out to all the students who graduated – you guys did great! 🎉👩‍🎓👨‍🎓

My journey as a teacher at LW Zurich began a little over a year ago. I had the opportunity to be part of the teaching crew for five batches – 3 in data science and 2 in web development. 🖥️📊 It has been an amazing ride, full of learning and connecting with so many amazing people. 🌟👥

As a data scientist myself, I’m always truly impressed by what the participants can come up with in just 2 project weeks at the end of the bootcamp. This time there was only one data science project done by our talented participants: Alex, Tanushri, David and Felipe. They created a real-time audio translator from Bengali to English and vice versa. Their project is called BhashaBridge and can be found here. 🌍🔊

Big thanks to Alicia, our city manager, and Sara, who organized all our events. You both did so much to make Le Wagon such a great place and it really made a difference for all of us. 🙏🌟

I had the wonderful opportunity to teach several workshops organized by Sara, and it was an outstanding experience. Collaborating with Sara and engaging with all the participants was amazing. I hope that everyone who attended these workshops was able to take away some valuable knowledge and skills. 💡📝

I wish everyone the very best. Even though we're going in different directions, I hope our paths will meet again soon. 🛤️💫

Published: 12 DEC 2023

I attended my 3rd Startup Weekend Switzerland last weekend 🚀

I got to work with some cool people: Shanelle, Aurélien and Oleg (and Michael who randomly bumped into our team pic 📸). We devised this idea called GuideME – an AI-powered mental health coach 🧠💡.

During our customer validation process, we noticed a lot of need and interest in using AI for mental health coaching, but some were hesitant about opening up to an AI 🤖❓. I'm convinced that AI can significantly aid those dealing with stress or anxiety, especially as a stopgap solution until they can consult a professional. This is particularly relevant considering that the demand for mental health support far exceeds the available resources.

As a data scientist myself, I’m always truly impressed by what the participants can come up with in just 2 project weeks at the end of the bootcamp. This time there was only one data science project done by our talented participants: Alex, Tanushri, David and Felipe. They created a real-time audio translator from Bengali to English and vice versa. Their project is called BhashaBridge and can be found here: https://lnkd.in/dsB9__Sh. 🌍🔊

Huge thanks to everyone for a great time and to Erik for the pic! 🙌
