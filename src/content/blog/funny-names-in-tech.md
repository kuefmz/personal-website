I want to take a moment to appreciate the humor of developers and the memorable names they give to projects.

During a meeting with my supervisor, we talked about croissants, soups, and ducks, none of them in the everyday sense. Technical communities often choose playful names for tools that solve very serious problems. Those names make complex systems easier to remember and give open-source projects a little personality.

The contrast is part of the charm: difficult engineering, careful research, and a name that makes the conversation unexpectedly funny.
