The final Le Wagon Zurich Demo Day closed a meaningful chapter.

I joined the teaching team a little over a year earlier and worked with five cohorts across data science and web development. The projects students produced after only a few intensive weeks continually impressed me.

The final cohort included BhashaBridge, a real-time audio translator between Bengali and English. It was a strong example of a team turning an ambitious idea into a concrete demonstration under tight constraints.

Beyond the classes, I had the opportunity to teach public workshops and meet people taking brave steps into new careers. Teaching made me a better engineer: it exposes vague thinking quickly and keeps the human purpose of technical work visible.

I am grateful to the students, teaching teams, and organizers who made the Zurich community what it was.
