One automation idea I would like to explore is a service that helps creators publish video across YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram.

The repetitive part is easy to recognize: adapting formats, preparing platform-specific metadata, scheduling posts, checking whether each upload succeeded, and recovering when one platform rejects the content.

## Automation before AI

The core product does not need to begin with an AI model. Reliable integrations, media processing, scheduling, status tracking, and clear error handling would already solve much of the problem.

AI might later help with bounded tasks such as drafting captions or suggesting descriptions. Those features should support the workflow rather than distract from whether publishing actually works.

## What needs validation

This is currently a concept, not a released solution. Before building it, I would want to understand which steps consume the most time, how creators currently organize their work, what the platform APIs allow, and where human approval is essential.

That distinction matters to me. Product development is not only having an idea and implementing it. It is learning whether the problem is real, who experiences it, and what the smallest genuinely useful solution could be.
