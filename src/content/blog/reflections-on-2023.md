2023 was a year of plans changing shape.

I began the year working on a research paper that was ultimately rejected. The experience was difficult, but the feedback sharpened the questions I wanted to answer. That work became the foundation for starting my PhD.

Living in Zurich also pushed me to return to language learning. I made progress in German and resumed Spanish, rediscovering how rewarding it is to be a beginner.

## Learning through teaching

Teaching data science bootcamps and workshops became an important part of my work. Supporting people who are changing careers or strengthening their technical skills is both demanding and motivating. It also makes me explain familiar ideas with greater precision.

I ran my first marathon in Amsterdam, a long-held goal that reinforced a simple lesson: consistency usually matters more than intensity.

The year had setbacks too. I had to accept that I could not pursue every goal at once, and that changing direction is not the same as failing. I entered 2024 with ambitious plans again, but with more room for them to evolve.
