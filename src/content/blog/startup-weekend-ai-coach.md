At my third Startup Weekend Switzerland, our team developed **GuideME**, an early concept for an AI-powered mental health coach.

The most valuable part was not building the prototype. It was customer validation.

People recognized the need for more accessible support, particularly while waiting to see a professional. At the same time, many were understandably hesitant to share sensitive thoughts with an AI system.

That tension is the product problem. Technical capability is only one part of a useful system; trust, privacy, boundaries, and clear expectations are just as important.

I remain interested in how AI might offer limited, responsible support for stress or anxiety without presenting itself as a replacement for professional care. The weekend was a compact reminder that product development begins with listening, not with the model.
