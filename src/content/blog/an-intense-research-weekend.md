This past weekend brought together several parts of my research life.

On Friday, I learned that the first paper of my PhD had been accepted. It was a workshop paper and a work in progress toward a larger journal contribution, but it marked an important step in shaping the research.

On Saturday, I presented **AI & NLP: The Rise of LLMs** at the interdisciplinary Bolyai Conference in Hungary. It was energizing to discuss these ideas with people from different fields and to translate a fast-moving technical topic for a broader expert audience.

The weekend was also a chance to reconnect with friends and former colleagues. Research can look solitary from the outside, but progress depends heavily on the communities that question, challenge, and encourage us.
